## Topic 1: French Verlan (Back Slang)
- **Slug:** verlan
- **Description:** Verlan is a French argot characterized by the reversal of syllables in words. Originally emerging from Parisian banlieues in the 1980s, it has evolved into a complex linguistic phenomenon used across social classes.
- **Why Underserved:** While many sites mention verlan superficially, few provide comprehensive historical evolution, linguistic mechanisms, or practical learning tools. Most coverage is anecdotal rather than systematic.

### Pillar Outlines:

1. **Overview (Pillar 1):**
   - Definition and basic concept of verlan
   - How verlan transforms standard French words
   - Current usage contexts (youth culture, music, media)
   - Basic examples: "femme" → "meuf", "lourd" → "relou"
   - Connection to French identity and urban culture

2. **History & Evolution (Pillar 2):**
   - Origins in 19th century French linguistic games
   - Emergence in 1980s Parisian suburbs (banlieues)
   - Spread through hip-hop and rap music (IAM, Suprême NTM)
   - Mainstream adoption in 1990s-2000s
   - Double-verlan phenomena (verlan du verlan)
   - Regional variations (Marseille, Lyon, Brussels)

3. **Technical Deep-Dive (Pillar 3):**
   - Phonological mechanisms of syllable reversal
   - Rules for consonant cluster handling
   - Vowel preservation and elision patterns
   - Prosody and stress shift in verlan
   - Morphological constraints (which words can/cannot be verlanized)
   - Comparison to other language games (Pig Latin, Rhyming Slang)

4. **Ontology & Knowledge Base (Pillar 4):**
   - Comprehensive glossary of common verlan terms
   - Categories: people descriptors, actions, objects, emotions
   - Frequency rankings based on usage data
   - Generational markers (which terms signal which era)
   - Social register indicators
   - Cross-reference to standard French equivalents

5. **Current Trends & Future Outlook (Pillar 5):**
   - Digital age verlan evolution
   - Social media influence on spread
   - Verlan in French cinema and Netflix content
   - Educational attitudes (prohibition vs. inclusion)
   - Predictions for verlan in 2030+
   - Global French verlan (Belgium, Switzerland, Quebec, Africa)

6. **Tools & Resources (Pillar 6):**
   - Interactive Verlan Translator (user inputs French word, gets verlan)
   - Reverse Verlan Decoder (deciphers verlan back to standard French)
   - Practice quiz with scoring
   - Difficulty-based word lists (beginner/intermediate/advanced)
   - Audio pronunciation guide

7. **Common Challenges & Solutions (Pillar 7):**
   - When NOT to use verlan (professional contexts)
   - Avoiding outdated terms that mark you as trying too hard
   - Regional variations that cause confusion
   - Understanding rapid verlan in movies/music
   - Common mistakes French learners make
   - Integration strategies for language students
